## Category: Billing & Payments

### How can I pay my GPL bill?
GPL accepts payments through multiple channels:
- GPL Offices (in-person)
- Post Offices
- Banks (participating locations)
- SurePay payment kiosks
- BillExpress service
- MMG Mobile (dial *123# â†’ PAY BILLS)
- Cheque Dropbox

### How do I view my bill online?
Visit **billing.gplinc.net** to access your GPL web account. You can monitor account activity, view account details, and check your balance online.

### What if my bill seems incorrect or too high?
First, check your meter reading and compare with your bill. If you believe there's an error:
1. Contact Customer Service: 226-2600 or WhatsApp 608-9090
2. For formal complaints, write to Customer Services Manager at 40 Main Street, Georgetown
3. Include: your name, account number, nature of complaint, and any previous responses

### What if I miss the meter reader?
Take a clear photo of your current meter reading and send it via WhatsApp to **592-608-8575**.

### How is my electricity bill calculated?
Your bill includes:
- Fixed charge (based on customer category)
- Energy charge (per kWh consumed)
- Any applicable taxes and surcharges
Visit gplinc.com/bill/understand-your-bill/ for detailed breakdown.

### Can I set up automatic payments?
Contact GPL Customer Service at 226-2600 to inquire about automatic payment options for your account.

### What happens if I don't pay my bill on time?
Late payments may result in:
- Late payment charges
- Service disconnection
- Reconnection fees
Contact GPL immediately if you're having difficulty paying to discuss payment arrangements.

## Category: New Service & Connections

### How do I apply for new electricity service?
1. Start your application at https://gplinc.com/services/
2. A Customer Service Representative will contact you to schedule an appointment
3. Visit the nearest GPL office with required documents
4. Sign application form and pay security deposit
5. GPL conducts site investigation

### What documents do I need for new service?
**Required for all applicants:**
- National Identification (ID) card

**Additional for new housing schemes:**
- Agreement of Sale with Government of Guyana, OR
- Allocation letter with receipts showing full payment

### How long does it take to get connected?
Connection time varies based on:
- Site location and accessibility
- Technical requirements
- Completion of site investigation
Contact GPL for estimated timeline for your specific situation.

### How much is the security deposit?
Security deposit amount depends on your customer category (residential, commercial, industrial). Contact GPL at 226-2600 for current rates.

### Can I transfer service when I move?
Yes, you can request a change of tenancy. Visit your nearest GPL office with:
- Your current account information
- New address details
- Identification documents

## Category: Outages & Emergencies

### How do I report a power outage?
Report outages through:
- **Call Centre**: 592 226 2600
- **WhatsApp**: 592 608 9090
- **Emergency Hotlines** (by region):
  - Demerara: 0475 or 226-2600
  - Berbice: 333-2186 or 333-2716
  - Essequibo: See regional numbers (623-4014, 623-5012, etc.)
- **Facebook**: @GPLIncorporated
- **Website LiveChat**: gplinc.com

### What should I do if I see a downed power line?
**CRITICAL SAFETY STEPS:**
1. Stay at least 35 feet away
2. Do NOT touch the line or anything touching it
3. Call 911 immediately
4. Call GPL emergency hotline for your region
5. Do NOT attempt to move the line
6. Keep others away from the area
7. Assume all downed lines are LIVE and dangerous

### How long will the power outage last?
Outage duration depends on:
- Cause of outage (storm, equipment failure, maintenance)
- Extent of damage
- Accessibility of affected area
Call GPL emergency hotline for estimated restoration time for your area.

### Is there planned maintenance in my area?
GPL announces planned maintenance and outages through:
- Official website notifications
- Facebook page
- SMS alerts (if subscribed)
Check gplinc.com or call 226-2600 for scheduled maintenance information.

### What should I do during a power outage?
- Turn off and unplug sensitive electronic equipment
- Keep refrigerator and freezer doors closed
- Use flashlights (not candles when possible)
- Report the outage to GPL
- Stay away from downed power lines
- Keep emergency numbers handy

## Category: Safety & Electrical Information

### Is it safe to work near power lines?
**ALWAYS call GPL at 226-2600 BEFORE working near power lines.** This includes:
- Tree trimming
- Construction work
- Installing antennas
- Ladder work
- Heavy equipment operation
Recent incidents highlight the dangers of unauthorized work near power lines.

### What should I do if my vehicle hits a power pole?
1. Stay in your vehicle if it's safe
2. Call 911 and GPL emergency hotline
3. Honk your horn to alert others
4. Do NOT exit unless there's fire or immediate danger
5. If you must exit: jump clear with both feet together, shuffle away

### How can I reduce my electricity bill?
**Energy Saving Tips:**
- Use LED bulbs instead of incandescent
- Unplug devices when not in use
- Use natural ventilation when possible
- Maintain air conditioning units regularly
- Use energy-efficient appliances
- Run major appliances during off-peak hours
- Seal air leaks in your home

### What is the difference between kW and kWh?
- **kW (Kilowatt)**: Measure of power (rate of energy use)
- **kWh (Kilowatt-hour)**: Measure of energy consumed over time
- Your bill is based on kWh consumed

### How do I read my electricity meter?
**For digital meters:** Record the numbers shown on display
**For dial meters:** Read dials from left to right, recording the number the pointer has passed
GPL provides meter reading guides at gplinc.com or call 226-2600 for assistance.

## Category: Technical Information

GPL supplies electricity at standard voltages:

Contact GPL for specific voltage information for your location.

Contact GPL's Renewable Energy department:

Generator size depends on:

Consult a qualified electrician for proper sizing.

- Engineering assessment
- New meter installation
- Electrical panel upgrade
- Additional security deposit

2. Provide final meter reading
3. Settle any outstanding balance
4. Return GPL property (if any)

Visit GPL office with:

3. Provide meter readings if relevant

## Category: Emergency Preparedness

### What should I keep in an emergency kit?
**Essential items:**
- Flashlights and extra batteries
- Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
- First aid kit
- Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
- Water (1 gallon per person per day)
- Medications
- Important documents
- Cash
- GPL emergency numbers written down

### How can I prepare for hurricane season?
**Before a storm:**
- Charge all devices
- Fill bathtubs with water
- Secure outdoor items
- Have emergency kit ready
- Know GPL emergency numbers
- Take photos of meter reading

**During a storm:**
- Stay indoors away from windows
- Avoid using corded phones
- Unplug sensitive electronics
- Do NOT go outside to check power lines

**After a storm:**
- Stay away from downed power lines
- Report outages to GPL
- Do NOT use generators indoors
- Be patient - crews work as quickly and safely as possible
