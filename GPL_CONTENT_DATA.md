## ðŸ¢ About Guyana Power & Light Inc.

### Company Overview
**GPL** is the largest supplier of electricity in Guyana, South America, with its franchise area encompassing all three counties of **Demerara, Berbice and Essequibo**. GPL is a publicly owned utility company in Guyana, providing electric power in the country. The company employs over **1,200 workers** countrywide with a multitude of industrial and administrative skills.

### Service Coverage
GPL's customer base extends from **Charity in Region 2** to **Moleson Creek in Region 6**, and powers the islands of **Leguan & Wakenaam** in the Essequibo River. GPL generates power, transmits and distributes it to most of the areas along the coastal plain of Guyana.

### Company History

**Early Development (1900s-1960s):**
- **Turn of 20th century**: International Power Company (IPC) of Canada operates in Georgetown
- **1925**: Demerara Electric Company established, purchases IPC assets
- **1957**: British Guiana Electricity Company (BGEC) formed
- **1960**: BGEC purchases Demerara Electric Company assets

**Post-Independence (1966-1999):**
- **May 1966**: After Independence, BGEC nationalized and Guyana Electricity Company (GEC) established, wholly owned by the state
- **Prior to October 1, 1999**: Company named Guyana Electricity Corporation, wholly owned by Government of Guyana

**Partnership Era (1999-2003):**
- **October 1, 1999**: 50/50 equity partnership established between Government of Guyana and a consortium comprising Commonwealth Development Corporation (CDC) of UK and Electricity Supply Board International (ESBI) of Ireland, creating GPL

**Return to State Ownership (2003-Present):**
- **April 2003**: Partnership dissolved, GPL reverted to 100% ownership by government and people of Guyana

## âš ï¸ Safety Information

### Downed Power Line Safety

**Critical Rules:**
1. **Stay Away**: Move at least 35 feet away from downed lines and anything touching them
2. **You Cannot Tell**: Power lines may be energized even if they appear inactive
3. **Call for Help**: Immediately call 911 and GPL emergency hotline
4. **Never Touch**: Do not touch anyone in contact with downed lines
5. **Never Move**: Do not attempt to move downed lines with any object (broom, stick, etc.)
6. **Avoid Water**: Do not touch or step in water near downed power lines
7. **Never Drive Over**: Do not drive over downed power lines

### If Your Vehicle Contacts a Power Line
- **Stay Inside**: Remain in the vehicle if it's safe
- **Honk for Help**: Use your horn to summon assistance
- **Exit Only If Fire**: If you must leave due to fire:
  - Jump out with both feet together
  - Avoid contact with car and ground simultaneously
  - Shuffle away (don't walk normally)

### Before Working Near Power Lines
**Always call GPL at 226-2600 before commencing any work near electrical infrastructure**. Recent incidents (January 2026) highlight ongoing concerns about reckless practices by contractors threatening grid safety and stability.
