## ‚ö° Downed Power Line Safety

### CRITICAL: Treat ALL Downed Power Lines as LIVE and DEADLY

**A downed power line can kill you instantly. You CANNOT tell if a line is energized just by looking at it.**

### If You See a Downed Power Line

#### DO:
‚úÖ **Stay at least 35 feet (10 meters) away** from the line and anything touching it
‚úÖ **Call 911 immediately**
‚úÖ **Call GPL emergency hotline** for your region
‚úÖ **Keep others away** - warn anyone approaching
‚úÖ **Stay on scene** safely to warn others until help arrives
‚úÖ **Assume the line is LIVE** - even if it's not sparking or moving

#### DO NOT:
‚ùå **DO NOT touch** the power line or anything touching it
‚ùå **DO NOT attempt to move** the line with any object (stick, broom, etc.)
‚ùå **DO NOT touch anyone** who is in contact with the line
‚ùå **DO NOT drive over** downed power lines
‚ùå **DO NOT touch or step in water** near downed lines
‚ùå **DO NOT assume** the line is dead because it's not sparking

### Ground Current Danger Zone
The ground around a downed power line can be energized up to **35 feet (10 meters) away**. Electricity travels through the ground in invisible pathways.

**If you're too close:**
- Keep feet together
- Shuffle away (don't walk normally)
- Do NOT run or take large steps

## üöó Vehicle Safety Near Power Lines

### If Your Vehicle Contacts a Power Line

#### Stay Inside If Safe:
1. **DO NOT exit** the vehicle
2. **Call 911** and GPL emergency hotline
3. **Honk your horn** continuously to alert others
4. **Warn others** to stay away from your vehicle
5. **Wait for utility crews** to de-energize the line

#### Exit Only If There's Fire:
1. **Open door** without touching metal parts
2. **Jump clear** of vehicle with both feet together
3. **Do NOT touch** vehicle and ground at same time
4. **Land with feet together**
5. **Shuffle away** - keep feet together, small steps
6. **Get at least 35 feet away**

### Why Shuffle?
Walking normally creates a "step potential" - different voltage points that electricity can travel through your body. Shuffling keeps your feet at the same voltage level.

## üå©Ô∏è Storm & Hurricane Safety

### Before a Storm

**Preparation:**
- Charge all electronic devices
- Have flashlights and batteries ready
- Fill bathtubs with water
- Have 3-day supply of non-perishable food
- Keep GPL emergency numbers written down
- Secure or bring inside outdoor items
- Photograph your meter reading
- Have emergency kit ready

**Electrical Safety:**
- Unplug sensitive electronics
- Turn off major appliances
- Know your circuit breaker location
- Have surge protectors on important equipment

### During a Storm

**DO:**
‚úÖ Stay indoors away from windows
‚úÖ Avoid using corded phones (cell phones are safe)
‚úÖ Unplug electronics if lightning is nearby
‚úÖ Stay away from water and plumbing
‚úÖ Keep refrigerator/freezer doors closed

**DO NOT:**
‚ùå Go outside to check on power lines
‚ùå Touch electrical equipment if wet
‚ùå Use candles near flammable materials
‚ùå Run generators indoors or in garages

### After a Storm

**Safety First:**
- Assume all downed power lines are LIVE
- Stay away from standing water (may be electrified)
- Don't touch wet electrical equipment
- Report downed lines to GPL and 911
- Check for gas leaks before using lights/electricity

**Reporting Outages:**
Call GPL emergency hotlines:
- Demerara: 0475 or 226-2600
- Berbice: 333-2186
- Essequibo: Regional numbers above

**Be Patient:**
- Restoration crews work as quickly and safely as possible
- Widespread damage takes time to repair
- Life-threatening situations are prioritized
- Damaged equipment must be replaced (can't be rushed)

## üè° Home Electrical Safety

### Preventing Electrical Fires

**Warning Signs:**
- Burning smell from outlets or appliances
- Discolored or warm outlet covers
- Flickering lights
- Frequent circuit breaker trips
- Buzzing sounds from electrical panel
- Sparks from outlets or switches

**If you notice these:** Turn off power at breaker and contact a qualified electrician immediately.

### Outlet & Appliance Safety

**DO:**
‚úÖ Use three-prong plugs as designed
‚úÖ Replace damaged cords immediately
‚úÖ Keep appliances away from water
‚úÖ Unplug appliances when not in use
‚úÖ Use surge protectors
‚úÖ Keep electrical panels accessible

**DO NOT:**
‚ùå Overload outlets or extension cords
‚ùå Use damaged electrical cords
‚ùå Run cords under rugs or furniture
‚ùå Use appliances with wet hands
‚ùå Remove third prong from plugs
‚ùå Use indoor appliances outdoors

### Water & Electricity - DEADLY COMBINATION

**Never mix water and electricity:**
- Don't touch electrical items with wet hands
- Keep appliances away from sinks, tubs
- Install GFCI outlets in bathrooms/kitchens
- Don't use electric devices near pools
- If an appliance falls in water: DON'T REACH FOR IT
  - Turn off power at breaker first
  - Unplug the appliance
  - Don't use until inspected by professional

## üîå Generator Safety

### Safe Generator Operation

**DO:**
‚úÖ **Operate outdoors only** - at least 20 feet from building
‚úÖ **Point exhaust away** from windows/doors
‚úÖ **Use transfer switch** or carefully manage circuits
‚úÖ **Let generator cool** before refueling
‚úÖ **Store fuel safely** in approved containers
‚úÖ **Follow manufacturer instructions**

**DO NOT:**
‚ùå **NEVER run generators indoors** - garages, basements, enclosed spaces
‚ùå **Carbon monoxide KILLS** - you can't see or smell it
‚ùå **Don't plug into wall outlets** - can back-feed and kill utility workers
‚ùå **Don't overload generator** - follow rated capacity
‚ùå **Don't refuel while running or hot**

### Carbon Monoxide Warning Signs
- Headache
- Dizziness
- Weakness
- Nausea
- Chest pain
- Confusion

**If you suspect CO poisoning:** Get outside immediately and call 911

- Equipment damage

**NEVER:**

- Use metal or wet tools near power lines
- Work from ladders near power lines
- Allow branches to fall on power lines

### Qualified Arborists Only
If GPL approves tree work near power lines, hire only:
- Licensed tree service companies
- Workers trained in electrical safety
- Companies with proper insurance
- Those who coordinate with GPL

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Emergency Situations

### Electrical Fire

**If safe to do so:**
1. Turn off power at breaker
2. Call 911
3. Use fire extinguisher rated for electrical fires (Class C)
4. Evacuate if fire spreads

**NEVER:**
- Use water on electrical fires
- Touch burning electrical equipment
- Attempt to fight large fires yourself

### Someone is Being Electrocuted

**DO NOT TOUCH THE PERSON**

1. **Cut power** at breaker if quickly accessible
2. **Call 911** immediately
3. **Call GPL** emergency hotline
4. **If person is free** from contact, begin CPR if trained
5. **Wait for help** - do not touch until power is confirmed off

### Flooding & Electricity

**If water is rising:**
- Turn off power at main breaker if safe
- Evacuate immediately
- Call GPL to report
- Don't return until authorized
- Have electrician inspect before restoring power

**Never:**
- Enter flooded areas with electricity on
- Touch electrical equipment while standing in water
- Assume power is off - verify with GPL

## üè• First Aid for Electrical Shock

### If Someone Receives Electric Shock

**1. DO NOT TOUCH THEM** until power is off

**2. Cut Power:**
- Turn off at breaker/fuse box, OR
- Unplug the device (don't touch victim)

**3. Call Emergency Services:**
- 911 for ambulance
- GPL emergency hotline

**4. If Victim Is Not Breathing:**
- Begin CPR if trained
- Use AED if available
- Continue until help arrives

**5. Treat Burns:**
- Don't remove clothing stuck to burns
- Cover burns with sterile dressing
- Don't apply ointments
- Keep victim warm

### Electrical Shock Can Cause:
- Heart stoppage
- Severe burns (internal and external)
- Muscle damage
- Nerve damage
- Broken bones (from fall or muscle contractions)

**Always seek medical attention** even if victim seems okay. Internal injuries may not be immediately apparent.
